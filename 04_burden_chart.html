Instruments counted per phase in which they are active. Some instruments (e.g. ACC Act, Te Ture Whenua Māori Act, Trusts Act) span multiple phases and are counted in each.
What this shows: The burden peaks at estate administration and succession — the two phases when whānau are simultaneously grieving and managing the most complex legal requirements, across the most government agencies. Ministry of Justice instruments dominate throughout, but IRD, MSD, Te Arawhiti, and LINZ each add distinct institutional touchpoints that families must navigate separately. There is no single agency through which the whole can be managed.
