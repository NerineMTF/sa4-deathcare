TIMELINE + DEPENDENCIES VIEW FOR FAMILY COORDINATOR
This is a simplified representation designed to test whether making sequence and dependencies
visible reduces cognitive load for the family coordinator.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
DAY 1-3 🔴 URGENT
(High emotional intensity/shock phase)
Registration deadline: 3 working days of burial or cremation

*   **Funeral decisions (can't delay)**: This involves making immediate choices regarding burial or cremation, often guided by the deceased's wishes as expressed in their Will. Engaging a funeral director is a priority.
*   **Medical certification of death (completed by doctor)**: The doctor or nurse practitioner will issue the Medical Certificate of Cause of Death (HP4720). This document is a prerequisite for the funeral director to proceed with the body and for the official registration of death.
*   **Provide accurate identity details for death registration and death certificate**: The funeral director typically handles the submission of these details to Births, Deaths and Marriages (BDM). The death must be registered within **3 working days of the burial or cremation** [1].

**Legal shifts that occur:**
⚠️ **Power of attorney ceases on death**: Any existing Enduring Power of Attorney (EPA) becomes immediately invalid upon death. **Critical Warning:** Using the deceased's bank cards, online banking, or other financial access after death, even for seemingly minor expenses, is legally problematic and should be avoided.
⚠️ **Bank accounts are typically frozen once notified**: While individual bank accounts are frozen for withdrawals upon notification of death, most New Zealand banks (e.g., ANZ, BNZ, Westpac, ASB) will pay the funeral director's invoice **directly** from the deceased's sole account if sufficient funds are available [2] [3] [4]. The family coordinator should provide the invoice to the bank.

**Time-sensitive (must happen now):**
✓ **Locate will**: This is crucial and should be a top priority. The Will often contains specific funeral wishes (e.g., burial vs. cremation, specific songs) that need to be known before finalising arrangements with the funeral director. It also identifies the Executor.
✓ **Gather ID documents (birth/marriage certificates)**: These are essential for death registration and later for proving identity during estate administration.
✓ **Digital "Kill Switch"**: Secure or memorialise social media accounts, email, and other digital assets to prevent misuse, hacking, or automated posts. This may involve contacting service providers directly.
✓ **Organ Donation Check**: If the deceased was a registered organ donor or had expressed wishes for organ donation, this decision is highly time-sensitive and must be discussed with medical staff immediately.
✓ **Home Security**: If the deceased lived alone, ensure the property is secured, manage perishable items (e.g., clear the fridge), and arrange care for any pets.
✓ **Employer Notification**: Inform the deceased's employer as soon as possible to stop payroll, inquire about any death in service benefits, or insurance policies.

**Not urgent but helpful:**
✓ **Contact lawyer**: If a lawyer was known to the deceased and is not the named executor, it can be helpful to inform them.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
WEEK 1-3 ⏸️ WAITING
(Death Certificate Processing Phase)
Typical processing: 1-3 weeks after registration

*   **Death certificate being processed**: This is the primary bottleneck in this phase. The Births, Deaths and Marriages (BDM) office processes the death registration and issues the official death certificate. Current processing times are approximately **8 working days** after registration, with most people receiving certificates within **1-2 weeks** [5].

🔒 **Without death certificate, you generally cannot:**
- Apply for probate
- Provide formal proof of death to banks, KiwiSaver providers, or insurance companies
- Lodge KiwiSaver/superannuation claims
- Update land title ownership
- Finalise IRD estate registration
- Claim life insurance

⚠️ **System appears paused, but multiple processes are waiting on this single document.** This is a period of high frustration for family coordinators due to perceived inactivity.

**What you can do now (Proactive Steps):**
✓ **Meet with lawyer to prepare probate documents**: Even without the death certificate, a lawyer can begin drafting the necessary documents for a probate application, saving time once the certificate is issued.
✓ **Compile asset and liability list**: Start gathering information on all assets (bank accounts, property, investments, vehicles) and liabilities (debts, loans, mortgages) of the deceased. This will be crucial for estate administration.
✓ **Notify banks (accounts may be frozen upon notification)**: While accounts are frozen, it is important to notify all financial institutions. This also allows for the funeral invoice payment process mentioned above.
✓ **Gather information for estate IRD number**: The estate will likely require its own IRD number for tax purposes. Gathering the necessary information now can expedite the application later.
✓ **Identify accounts, KiwiSaver, insurance policies**: Consolidate information on all financial holdings and policies.
✓ **Organise multiple certified copies of death certificate once issued**: You will need several certified copies for various institutions. Order these as soon as the death certificate is available.
✓ **Utilise myTrove**: Consider using **myTrove** (mytrove.co.nz), a free service that allows you to notify multiple organisations (e.g., Department of Internal Affairs, IRD, banks, insurers) of the death simultaneously, reducing repetitive administrative tasks [6].

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
MONTH 1 📋 LEGAL PREPARATION PHASE
(Proof & Authority Preparation Phase)
Timeline: Weeks 1-4

*   **Death certificate issued**: This unlocks the ability to provide formal proof of death to various institutions.

**Allows formal proof of death to be provided to:**
- Probate court
- Banks
- KiwiSaver / superannuation providers
- Insurance providers
- IRD (for estate registration and tax matters)

*   **Lawyer prepares and files probate application (Executor authority must be legally confirmed)**: If required, the executor, with legal assistance, will apply to the High Court for a Grant of Probate (if there is a Will) or Letters of Administration (if there is no Will). This legally confirms the executor's authority to manage the estate.

🔒 **Until probate is granted, executors generally cannot:**
- Access or transfer significant estate assets
- Sell property belonging to the estate
- Distribute funds to beneficiaries

**CRITICAL UPDATE: Probate Threshold Increase & Partial Access**
As of **September 24, 2025**, the threshold for requiring probate in New Zealand increased from $15,000 to **$40,000** [7] [8]. This means that if the total value of assets held at any single institution (e.g., a specific bank account, a KiwiSaver fund) is under $40,000, that institution may release the funds directly to the executor or next of kin **without requiring a formal Grant of Probate**. This significantly reduces the need for probate for many smaller estates and can accelerate the administration process for family coordinators.

⏱️ **What you're waiting for:**
Probate court processing. The Ministry of Justice has an internal standard of completing **75% of probate applications within 15 working days** (approximately 3 weeks) [9]. However, the average case takes **4-6 weeks** [10]. Complex estates may take longer.

⚠️ **This phase often feels invisible**: Documents are moving through the legal system, but there may be no material change or access to assets for the family coordinator.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
MONTH 2-3 ⏸️ PROBATE PROCESSING PHASE
(Authority Pending Phase)
Timeline: Weeks 4-12 (approximately 4-6 weeks from filing)

*   **Probate court processes application independently of family timeline.** The High Court works through applications, and the family has limited influence over this process.

🔒 **Until probate is granted, executors generally cannot (for assets over $40,000 per institution):**
- Transfer estate assets exceeding the $40,000 threshold
- Close or access full bank accounts
- Sell property
- Distribute funds to beneficiaries

✓ **Partial Access May Be Available:**
- Assets under $40,000 at any single institution may be released without probate [7]
- This can include some bank accounts, smaller KiwiSaver balances, or insurance payouts
- Contact each institution directly to inquire about releases under the $40,000 threshold

⚠️ **Executor holds legal responsibility for the estate but lacks full legal authority to transact.** This can be a period of significant stress and perceived stagnation for the family coordinator.

**Actions possible during this phase (Proactive Steps):**
✓ **Prepare tax documentation**: Begin gathering all necessary financial records for the deceased for their final income tax return and for the estate.
✓ **Confirm debts and liabilities**: Identify and verify all outstanding debts and financial obligations of the deceased.
✓ **Track down additional assets**: Continue to search for any assets that may not have been initially identified.
✓ **Maintain communication with beneficiaries**: Keep beneficiaries informed of the progress, even if it feels slow.
✓ **Consult accountant (if required) regarding estate tax obligations**: For complex estates, professional tax advice may be necessary.
✓ **Apply for Estate IRD number**: While not always requiring probate, having the death certificate allows for the application of a separate IRD number for the estate, which is crucial for managing estate income and tax obligations.

⏱️ **Delay length is variable and largely outside family control.** Most applications are processed within 4-6 weeks, but complex estates or High Court backlogs can extend this period.
⚠️ **This phase often feels stagnant**: Responsibility remains, but tangible progress in asset distribution is limited.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
MONTH 2-3 💰 ACCESS PHASE
(Authority Activated Phase)
Timeline: Weeks 4-12 (once probate is granted)

*   **Probate granted → Executor authority confirmed**: The High Court issues the Grant of Probate (or Letters of Administration), legally empowering the executor to manage and distribute the estate.

**UNLOCKS ability to:**
- Access and close bank accounts (including those over $40,000)
- Transfer or sell assets (including property)
- Progress KiwiSaver / superannuation release (if not already done under the $40,000 threshold)
- Finalise estate IRD registration and tax matters

**Administration now accelerates:**
- Asset liquidation (e.g., selling property, shares)
- Debt settlement (paying off creditors)
- Estate tax calculations and finalisation

🔒 **Distribution to beneficiaries is typically delayed to manage legal risk.**
⏱️ **Executors often wait up to 6 months from date of death before distributing assets** to reduce the risk of creditor claims or claims under the Family Protection Act 1955 [11]. This waiting period protects the executor from personal liability.
✓ **Place a "Notice to Creditors"**: To further mitigate risk, the executor can place a formal notice in a local newspaper under Section 35 of the Trustee Act 1956 or Section 35 of the Administration Act 1969, inviting creditors to make claims within a specified period.

⚠️ **Authority is now present but executor carries full legal risk.** Careful adherence to legal requirements is paramount.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
MONTH 6+ ✓ DISTRIBUTION
(Closure Phase)
Timeline: Approximately 6 months from date of death onwards

*   **Creditors paid**: All legitimate debts and liabilities of the estate are settled.
*   **IRD obligations settled**: This includes filing the deceased individual's final income tax return and the estate's income tax return (IR6) for any income earned by the estate after death [12].
*   **Final estate tax return filed**: All tax matters related to the estate are concluded.
*   **Beneficiaries receive distribution**: Assets are distributed to beneficiaries according to the Will or the laws of intestacy.
*   **Estate bank accounts closed**: All bank accounts opened for the estate are formally closed.
*   **Estate legal entity wound up**: The administration of the estate is formally concluded.

✓ **Executor responsibility formally concludes.**
⚠️ **Often accompanied by relief, exhaustion, and delayed emotional processing.** This marks the end of a long and often arduous administrative journey.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
KEY:
🔴 = Fast/Urgent timeline
⏸️ = Waiting period
🔒 = Blocked until prerequisite met
✓ = Actions you can take now
⏱️ = Normal/expected delay

## SUMMARY: REALISTIC TIMELINE FOR FAMILY COORDINATORS

| Phase | Timeline | Key Milestone | Status |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Immediate** | Day 1-3 | Funeral arranged, death registered | 🔴 Urgent |
| **Early Waiting** | Week 1-3 | Death certificate received | ⏸️ Waiting |
| **Preparation** | Month 1 | Probate application filed | 📋 Preparing |
| **Processing** | Month 2-3 | Probate granted; partial access available | ⏸️ Processing |
| **Access** | Month 2-3 | Full asset access (once probate granted) | 💰 Active |
| **Distribution** | Month 6+ | Assets distributed to beneficiaries | ✓ Closure |

**Important Note:** The $40,000 probate threshold (as of September 24, 2025) means that many smaller estates can access funds in Month 1-2 without waiting for probate. This significantly reduces the administrative burden for family coordinators managing smaller estates.
